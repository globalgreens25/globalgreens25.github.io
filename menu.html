Appetizers

Vegetarian Samosas (India)

This flaky, crisp pastry is prepared with all-purpose flour and stuffed with a savory filling made of mashed potatoes, green peas, onions, ginger, lentils, and green chili.

Zaalouk (Morocco)

$10

This vibrant and flavorsome dip, served with thick slices of bread, is made with tomatoes, eggplants, garlic cloves, parsley, coriander, cumin seeds, and lemon juice.

Mercimek Köftesi (Turkey)

$10

These traditional Turkish patties are made with finely chopped white and green onions, tomatoes, red peppers, red lentils, fine bulgur, parsley, cumin, and Aleppo pepper.

Farinata (Italy)

$10

This savory and gluten-free chickpea pancake is a popular Italian street food made with chickpea flour, olive oil, fine sea salt, fresh rosemary, and thin slices of yellow onion.

Gomen Wat (Ethiopia)

$10

This iconic Ethiopian side dish, served with injera (a popular Ethiopian flatbread), is made with collard greens, vegetable oil, ginger, garlic, red onion, smoked paprika, cumin, finely chopped tomatoes, and diced jalapenos.

Entrées

Japchae (Korea)

$18

These vibrant Korean sweet potato noodles are made with Dangmyun (glass noodles from sweet potato starch), spinach, garlic cloves, thin slices of onion, diced mushrooms, yellow bell peppers, carrots, and vegetable oil.

Mujadara (Lebanon)

$18

This classic and high-protein Lebanese dish consists of basmati rice, olive oil, thin slices of yellow onion, brown lentils, and cumin.

Fasolada (Greece)

$18

This traditional and comforting greek soup is prepared with celery, yellow onion, carrots, garlic cloves, paprika, white kidney beans, and tomatoes.

Moqueca de Palmito (Brazil)

$18

This popular and potassium-rich Brazilian stew is made with tomatoes, red and yellow bell peppers, coriander, coconut milk, olive oil, and hearts of palm (a vegetable native to South and Central America).

Tacu Tacu (Peru)

$18

This creamy Peruvian dish with a crispy exterior consists of diced red onions, garlic cloves, olive oil, long-grain rice, ají amarillo paste (yellow chili), lime juice, and Mayocoba beans.

$12

This traditional and chewy Japanese dessert from Kyoto is prepared with cinnamon powder, sweet red bean paste, matcha, shiratamako (sweet rice flour), joshinko (Japanese rice flour), and kinako (roasted soybean flour).

Plantain Mosa (Nigeria)

$12

This iconic Nigerian dessert with a golden brown exterior is made with plantains, semolina flour, yeast, baking powder, sugar, and vegetable oil.

Rote Grütze (Germany)

$12

This sweet and tart pudding from Northern Germany consists of cherry juice, granulated sugar, vanilla beans, cornstarch, and various berries (strawberries, raspberries, blueberries, and blackberries).

Nian Gao (China)

$12

This chewy and sugary cake, served in Chinese households during Lunar New Year, is prepared with rice flour, ginger, brown sugar, coconut milk, canola oil, and red dates.
